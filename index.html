What is Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation?

TMS uses magnetic pulses to stimulate specific areas of the brain responsible for mood regulation. It's FDA-cleared for depression, OCD, and anxiety—offering hope when medication hasn't worked.

During a 20-minute session, a small coil placed on your scalp generates painless magnetic pulses that activate underactive brain cells. You remain fully awake and can return to normal activities immediately after.

Non-invasive, no anesthesia
20-min sessions, 5 days/week
No side effects

Most patients report a tapping or clicking sensation on the scalp during treatment. It's generally well-tolerated and doesn't require anesthesia. Some patients find it slightly uncomfortable initially but adapt quickly.
A typical TMS treatment course consists of daily sessions (5 days a week) for 4-6 weeks. Each session lasts about 20 minutes. You're awake and alert throughout, and can return to normal activities immediately.
TMS has minimal side effects compared to medication. The most common are mild scalp discomfort or headache during or after treatment, which typically resolves quickly. There's no weight gain, drowsiness, sexual dysfunction, or other systemic effects.
TMS is often recommended for those with treatment-resistant depression (people who haven't responded well to medication) or those who experience significant side effects from antidepressants. Contact us for a free consultation to determine if TMS is right for you.
Response times vary. Some patients begin noticing improvements within 2-3 weeks, while others may take longer. Most patients experience full benefits by the end of their treatment course. Our team monitors your progress throughout.
